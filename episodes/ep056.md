Let's break down what each one is doing.

B.ai came out swinging with a simple message: every model, half off. They're positioning as crypto-native with anonymous blockchain wallet login, but they also support regular payment methods. The pitch is aggressive — original APIs, no wrappers, no throttling, Anthropic, OpenAI, Gemini, all at fifty percent off.

They support around forty models including DeepSeek, Claude, GPT, Gemini, and Chinese models like Kimi and Qwen. His message on Twitter was essentially: we're a real company, we don't cut corners, all models are direct contracts.

So what does this mean for the market?

First, the obvious: this is massive validation. When people with millions of followers and deep pockets enter your space, it means the market is real. The AI API gateway category just got a huge signal boost. More developers will search for "AI API gateway" this month than any month before. That's good for everyone in the space.

Second, let's talk about the celebrity playbook. We've seen this pattern before — in crypto exchanges, in VPN services, in hosting. A celebrity enters, subsidizes heavily to acquire users, generates massive buzz, and then one of two things happens: either they build a real product team and sustain it, or the subsidies dry up and users migrate to whoever actually has better infrastructure.

The "half off everything" model is essentially a customer acquisition cost. Justin Sun is paying the difference out of pocket to build a user base. The question every developer should ask is: what happens in six months when that promotion ends?

Third, there are specific weaknesses in celebrity-driven platforms that developer-focused tools don't have. Developer trust is earned through uptime, not tweets. When your production app goes down at two AM, you need responsive support — not a viral thread. Model freshness matters too. When a new model drops, how fast does it appear on the platform? And latency — are requests being routed optimally, or through whatever infrastructure was cheapest to set up quickly?

Here's the strategic insight for developers: use the attention flood to your advantage. These celebrities are spending millions educating the market about what an AI gateway is and why you need one. They're creating demand. But demand doesn't automatically flow to whoever is loudest — it flows to whoever delivers the best experience once a developer actually tries the product.

The long game in infrastructure is always the same: reliability, pricing transparency, and developer experience. Subsidized pricing attracts trial users. Consistent uptime and great docs convert them into long-term customers.

So if you're evaluating AI gateways right now — and there's never been more options — here's what to actually test: run the same prompt through multiple gateways and measure latency. Check if they support the latest model versions on day one. Read their status page history. Try their support channel. And most importantly, check what happens to your bill when the promotional period ends.
