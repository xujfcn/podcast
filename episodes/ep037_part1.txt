Full-blown agents that can plan, write, test, and iterate on code autonomously. Let's break down what's out there and what actually works.
If you're still thinking of AI coding tools as fancy autocomplete, you're about two years behind. The landscape has shifted dramatically. We've gone from "suggest the next line" to "here's a full feature implementation with tests."
Cursor kicked this off by embedding AI deeply into the editor experience. Then Windsurf came along with Cascade, their agentic workflow that can handle multi-file changes. GitHub Copilot evolved from suggestions to a full agent mode. And now we've got newer players like Kiro from Amazon, Augment Code, and others pushing the boundaries even further.
The key shift is autonomy. These tools don't just respond to prompts — they plan, execute, verify, and iterate. They read your codebase, understand your patterns, and make changes across multiple files in a single flow.
Let's do a quick comparison of the big three.
Cursor is the most mature agentic IDE. It has deep context awareness, can read your entire project, and its composer mode lets you describe a feature and watch it build across files. The tab completion is still best in class. Pricing is 20 dollars a month for Pro.
Windsurf, from Codeium, takes a different approach with Cascade. It's more conversational — you describe what you want, and it creates a step-by-step plan before executing. It's particularly good at understanding project structure and making coordinated changes. Their free tier is generous, and Pro is 15 dollars a month.
